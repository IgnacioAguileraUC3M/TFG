

      Mobile cellular services have spread much faster than anticipated  enabling people living in previously unconnected areas to join the global information society. By 2016  second-generation (2G) mobile telephony was almost universal  with 95 per cent of the worlds population covered. Third-generation (3G) mobile coverage  which delivers higher speed access to the Internet and information and communication technology services and applications  stood at 84 per cent globally. In LDCs  about 85 per cent of people lived in areas covered by a mobile cellular signal and 52 per cent lived in areas covered by a 3G signal.
