

      The share of renewable energyderived from hydropower  solid and liquid biofuels  the wind  sun  biogas  geothermal and marine sources  and wastein the worlds total final energy consumption increased marginally  from 17.4 per cent in 2000 to 18.1 per cent in 2012. However  modern renewables  which exclude solid biofuels  grew at a rate of 4 per cent a year between 2010 and 2012. The contribution of renewables to the electricity sector has been growing significantly. Modern renewables accounted for 60 per cent of all new power-generating capacity in 2014. In absolute terms  about 72 per cent of the increase in energy consumption from modern renewable sources between 2010 and 2012 came from developing regions  mostly Eastern Asia.
