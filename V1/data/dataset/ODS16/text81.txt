

    Worldwide  the proportion of people held in detention without being sentenced for a crime decreased only slightly over the last decadefrom 32 per cent of total detainees in 2003-2005 to 30 per cent in 2012-2014. The figure for developing regions has on average been higher than that for developed regions. The highest rate of unsentenced detainees was in Southern Asia  where  despite recent progress  about two out of three prisoners remained unsentenced in 2012-2014. In Latin America and the Caribbean  the region with the second highest percentage  the share of persons held in detention without being sentenced remained practically unchanged over the last decade  at 43 per cent.
